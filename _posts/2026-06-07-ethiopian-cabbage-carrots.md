One of my last home cooking challenges is to master Ethiopian food. It has been nearly 10 years since I lived in the Phoenix area where I could get it, and I miss it terribly. So, this is the first in a number of recipes I will be working on. Like most, it will not even attempt to be authentic, just to capture what I liked most about what I used to eat. This is a simple side dish of cabbage and carrots, flavored with turmeric and ginger.

### Ingredients

- 3 tablespoons cooking oil
- 1 onion, thinly sliced lengthwise
- 2 large carrots, peeled and cut into thin sticks
- 1 tablespoon garlic paste
- 1 tablespoon ginger paste
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 head green cabbage, shredded
- 2 small potatoes, peeled and cut into small cubes

### Directions

1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until soft. Add carrots and continue to cook until onions begin to get color.
1. Add the garlic and ginger paste, salt, pepper, turmeric, and cumin. Stir to coat the vegetables with the spices and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
1. Add cabbage and about 1/2 cup water. Cover and cook until cabbage is soft, about 15 minutes.
1. Add the potatoes with a little more water if needed and cook until tender, about 20 minutes more.
1. Adjust seasoning to taste and serve with injera.
